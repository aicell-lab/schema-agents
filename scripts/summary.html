Assessment of Hematological Biomarkers in a Prospective Cohort

Materials: Human subjects with occupational lead exposure, Control group of healthy individuals, Blood samples, Hematological analysis equipment, Logistic regression software

Protocol: Recruit subjects with documented occupational lead exposure and healthy controls, Collect blood samples from all subjects, Perform comprehensive hematological analysis, Develop a new logistic regression model incorporating novel biomarkers, Validate the model using ROC curve analysis

Expected Results: Identification of new hematological biomarkers with predictive value for lead poisoning

Elucidating the Role of NRF2 in Neuronal Defense Mechanisms

Materials: Human neural progenitor cells (NPCs), Lead (Pb) and arsenic (As) compounds, NRF2 inhibitors and activators, ChIP-qPCR assay materials, Gene expression analysis tools

Protocol: Culture NPCs and expose them to Pb and As, Apply NRF2 inhibitors and activators to assess their impact on gene expression, Perform ChIP-qPCR to confirm NRF2 binding on target gene promoters, Analyze gene expression changes and correlate with neuronal cell function assays, Compare gene expression profiles with those from ASD patient-derived NPCs

Expected Results: Understanding of NRF2's role in protecting NPCs from metal-induced damage

Evaluating the Protective Effects of Asparagus officinalis on Lead-Induced Hepatotoxicity

Materials: Male mice, Lead acetate (LA), Asparagus officinalis extract (AOE), Kidney function assay kits, Antioxidant assay kits, Inflammatory cytokine assay kits

Protocol: Divide male mice into four groups: control, LA exposure, LA exposure with AOE pretreatment, and AOE only, Administer LA and AOE according to group assignment, Assess kidney function and biomarkers at multiple time points, Measure antioxidant levels and inflammatory cytokines, Perform histopathological examination of kidney tissues

Expected Results: Confirmation of AOE's nephroprotective effects against lead-induced renal toxicity
